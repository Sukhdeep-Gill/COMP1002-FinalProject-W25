Our Story

Founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Jef Raskin and Bill Atkinson, Apple Inc. has been a pioneer in personal computing. In 1984, we revolutionized the industry with the introduction of the Macintosh, a user-friendly computer featuring a graphical interface, icons, and a mouseâ€”transforming personal computing forever.

The original Macintosh 128K, introduced on January 24, 1984, featured a 9-inch monochrome display and 128 KB of RAM. It was designed to be accessible to everyday users, not just experts and the first computer to use a graphical user interface and a mouse.

Over the years, the Macintosh line has evolved, introducing models like the Macintosh 512K and Macintosh Plus, each bringing innovations that set new standards in the industry.

Today, the Macintosh remains a testament to our commitment to innovation, design, and user-friendly technology, continuing to influence the computing world and Macs are currently sold with Apple's UNIX based macOS operating system.

What is Mac?

Mac is a brand of personal computers designed and marketed by Apple.
