Apple I (1976)

Founders: Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne

Price: $666.66

Features:

  • CPU: MOS 6502 @ 1 MHz
  • Memory: 4 or 8 KB
  • Storage: 256 B ROM.
  • Graphics: 40×24 characters, hardware-implemented scrolling.

Significance: It was Apple's first product and one of the first personal computers. Only around 200 units were made.

Apple II (1977)

Price: $1,298

Features:

  • CPU: 6502 @ 1.023 MHz (II, II Plus, IIe)
  • Storage :5.25 inch floppy disk, 3.5 inch floppy disk
  • Display: NTSC video out (built-in RCA connector)

Significance: One of the best-selling personal computers of the late 1970s and early 1980s.

Apple III (1980)

Price: $4567.98

Features:

  • Memory: 128 KB of RAM, expandable to 512 KB
  • Removable storage: 5+1⁄4 inch floppy disk
  • Display: 80×24 text.
    • 560×192 pixels (monochrome).
    280×192 pixels (16 colors or grayscale).
  • Sound 6-bit DAC

Significance: Faced technical issues and failed commercially, but contributed to the development of future Apple products.

Lisa (1983)

Price: $9,999.76

Features:

  • Operating system: Lisa OS, Xenix[2]
  • CPU:Motorola 68000 @ 5 MHz
  • Memory: 1 MB RAM, 16 KB Boot ROM
  • Display:12 in (30 cm) monochrome 720×364

Significance: Pioneered GUI concepts that were later refined in the Macintosh.

Macintosh (1984)

Price: $2,398.87

Features:

  • Iconic all-in-one design with a built-in 9-inch display.
  • 128KB of RAM, Motorola 68000 processor.
  • Came with a mouse and introduced the graphical user interface to the masses.

Significance: Revolutionized the computer industry and established Apple as an innovator in personal computing.

Macintosh Portable (1989)

Price: $6,500.89

Features:

  • One of Apple's first battery-powered computers.
  • Display: 9.8″ black and white active matrix LCD screen
  • Graphics: 640 × 400 pixel, 1-bit

Significance: Considered the precursor to modern laptops.

PowerBook Series (1991)

Price: $2,298.76

Features:

  • First laptops with a modern layout, including a trackball and palm rests.
  • CPU: Motorola 68000, Motorola 68030, Motorola 68LC040
  • Memory:2–40 MB
  • Storage: 20–500 MB

Significance: Defined the form factor for laptops that is still used today.
